# B3 — Brand Identity

> This is the single source of truth for brand decisions: master brand architecture, sub-brand rules, positioning, personality, voice, visual direction, and what to avoid.

---

## Master Brand: B3

**B3** is the company and the master brand. Everything else sits under it.

**B3** stands for **Buy · Build · Bring** — the three actions every construction project depends on: buying materials, building something, and bringing it all together with logistics.

The master brand is always **B3**. Sub-brands append a descriptor (see Brand Architecture below).

---

## Brand Architecture

B3 operates three distinct verticals under one master brand. Each has its own name, audience, and go-to-market — but they share the B3 visual identity, color system, and the B3Hub transaction platform as the connective tissue.

```
B3  (master brand — the company)
├── B3Hub          — digital marketplace platform
├── B3 Laukumi     — physical fulfillment network
└── B3 Būvniecība  — construction services
```

### Why sub-brands and not one monolithic name

The three verticals reach different people through different channels:

| Sub-brand         | Primary audience                                         | How they discover it                    |
| ----------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| **B3Hub**         | Construction companies, contractors, suppliers, carriers | App stores, digital ads, referrals      |
| **B3 Laukumi**    | Homeowners, micro-contractors, small builders            | Yard signage, Google Maps, driving past |
| **B3 Būvniecība** | Project owners, developers, municipalities               | Google search, tenders, site referrals  |

A homeowner driving past a gravel yard reads "B3 Laukumi" — immediately clear in Latvian, no explanation needed. That same homeowner does not need to know what a hub, marketplace, or platform is. A construction manager searching for a demolition quote searches "būvniecība", not "hub".

### Why not fully separate brands

B3 Laukumi IS B3Hub in the physical world. Every transaction at a B3 Laukumi location flows through the B3Hub platform — same payment, same documents, same data model. If they looked like unrelated companies, that connection (and the moat it creates) would be invisible. The shared B3 mark is what ties them together in the customer's mind and in the product.

### The rule: B3Hub is always the connective tissue

Even when a user only knows "B3 Laukumi", they interact with B3Hub under the hood. Physical locations must carry: _"darbojas uz B3Hub platformas"_ (powered by B3Hub platform). This is not optional — it's how the physical network feeds the digital platform's data and transaction volume.

### B3 Būvniecība is the most independent

Construction services (earthworks, demolition, drainage, landscaping) have a different sales cycle, different staff, and different risk profile from a transaction marketplace. They earn the most separation. B3 Būvniecība can win tenders entirely on its own merit without the buyer needing to know or care about the platform side. But the B3 mark on the door still earns trust from contractors who know B3Hub.

## Brand Positioning

> **B3Hub is the infrastructure layer for construction logistics in the Baltics.**

Not a marketplace. Not a procurement tool. Not a trucking app.
The infrastructure layer — the thing underneath everything else that moves materials from quarry to site.

### One-sentence pitch

> "Order materials, arrange transport, and dispose of waste — all in one transaction."

### Why this positioning wins

Most Latvian construction companies juggle 3–5 suppliers, 2–3 transport contacts, and paper-based waste certificates. B3Hub collapses that into a single trackable flow. The moat is not the software — it's the **physical B3 Field network** backed by the software. Competitors can copy the app; they can't copy the locations.

## Target Audiences & How to Talk to Each

### 1. Construction companies & contractors (primary buyers)

- They care about: time saved, cost per tonne, documentation compliance, no surprises on delivery
- Speak to them about: project cost control, automatic CMR/waste certificates, supplier benchmarks
- Never say: "seamless", "end-to-end solution", "ecosystem"
- Say instead: "You know exactly what each tonne costs before the truck leaves."

### 2. Quarries & material suppliers (sellers)

- They care about: more orders, faster payment, less paperwork, no bad-debt risk
- Speak to them about: new buyer reach, Stripe payouts, no invoicing chasing
- Never say: "digital transformation"
- Say instead: "Your prices. Your materials. Buyers find you."

### 3. Trucking companies & independent drivers (carriers)

- They care about: steady work, fast payment, no empty trucks on return
- Speak to them about: consistent jobs, backhaul matching, mobile-first job management
- Never say: "gig economy", "flexible opportunities"
- Say instead: "Less time on the phone. More time on the road."

### 4. Homeowners & micro-contractors (B3 Field users)

- They care about: getting 1–3 tonnes of gravel without needing a freight account
- Speak to them about: drive up, load, go — no minimum order, no haggling
- Tone: accessible, no jargon, practical
- Say instead: "Half a tonne of sand, ready to collect. No truck needed."

## Brand Personality

| Dimension     | B3Hub is                                         | B3Hub is NOT                                  |
| ------------- | ------------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------- |
| **Tone**      | Direct, factual, efficient                       | Salesy, hype-driven, corporate                |
| **Character** | The reliable contractor, not the startup founder | Cool, disruptive, visionary                   |
| **Language**  | Numbers first — tonnes, km, €, minutes           | Abstract benefits ("transform your workflow") |
| **Register**  | Professional B2B, approachable                   | Formal/legal OR too casual                    |
| **Energy**    | Calm confidence                                  | Urgency, pressure, FOMO                       |

The brand should feel like the best subcontractor you've ever worked with: shows up on time, does exactly what was agreed, sends the paperwork without being asked.

## Voice & Copy Rules

1. **Lead with the concrete outcome**, not the feature.
   - ❌ "Our platform automatically generates CMR documents."
   - ✅ "Your CMR is ready before the driver leaves the loading point."

2. **Use real units.** Construction people think in tonnes, m³, km, and euros.
   - ❌ "Efficiently manage your material orders."
   - ✅ "Track 500 tonnes across 12 sites. One screen."

3. **Latvian first for the Latvian market.** B3Hub communications in Latvia are in Latvian by default. English is for investor/partner communication.

4. **Short sentences win.** Baltic B2B buyers don't read paragraphs.

5. **Never use**: _seamless_, _end-to-end_, _ecosystem_, _holistic_, _synergy_, _leverage_, _empower_, _innovative_, _game-changing_, _disruptive_, _cutting-edge_.

## Visual Identity Direction

### Logomark concept

The **B3** letterform paired with a minimal node/hub motif — suggests connection and logistics without being a truck or a box (too literal, too cargo-app).

Consider: a compact geometric "B3" where the "3" forms a subtle arrow or movement cue.

### Color System

| Role                | Color                    | Rationale                                                            |
| ------------------- | ------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Primary**         | Deep amber `#E8860A`     | Construction, energy, action — distinctive in the Baltic B2B market  |
| **Dark base**       | Near-black `#0F0F0F`     | Heavy industry, seriousness, premium; avoids pure black flatness     |
| **Surface**         | Warm off-white `#FAF8F5` | Concrete, stone, natural materials — not clinical white              |
| **Accent**          | Forest green `#2D6A4F`   | Waste/recycling stream, sustainability, B3 Field environmental angle |
| **Data/UI neutral** | Slate `#64748B`          | Data tables, secondary text, infrastructure feel                     |

**Primary palette in use**: Amber on dark base for marketing/hero contexts. Amber + white on slate for app UI.

Avoid: blue-dominated palettes (too generic fintech), pure orange (too Schüttflix-clone), bright green (too eco-startup).

### Typography

| Role                          | Recommendation                                                                                        |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Display / Hero**            | [Syne](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Syne) — geometric, strong, Baltic modernist feel             |
| **Body / UI**                 | [Inter](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Inter) — universal legibility, trusted in construction SaaS |
| **Numerics / Data**           | Inter numerals with tabular figures — critical for price/weight tables                                |
| **Monospace (receipts/docs)** | JetBrains Mono — for generated documents, order IDs, CMR numbers                                      |

Display should feel structural and grounded — not rounded/friendly, not overly condensed industrial.

### Photography / Imagery Direction

**Use:**

- Real Latvian construction sites — not stock photography with perfect lighting
- Materials close-up (aggregate texture, wet sand, crushed stone)
- Truck cabins, loading, drivers — real work
- Aerial views of B3 Field locations

**Avoid:**

- Happy handshakes in boardrooms
- Glowing UI on MacBooks in coffee shops
- Generic European "smart city" stock
- Anything that looks like it could be a bank ad

## Brand in the Physical World (B3 Fields)

The B3 Field physical locations are the brand's strongest asset. Every B3 Field must be immediately recognizable.

### Physical branding principles

- **Amber and near-black** signage — visible from a distance on a construction site
- "B3 Field" is the location type name, always in this format, never "B3Hub Field" or "B3 Center"
- The entrance gate or sign should display the B3 logo, opening hours, and a QR code linking to the active location on B3Hub
- Weighbridge / scale receipts printed with B3Hub header and transaction ID — every tonne documented

## What B3 Is NOT Branded As

These are brand anti-patterns. If a design or copy direction starts trending toward these, it's wrong.

| Anti-pattern                   | Why it's wrong                                                                                          |
| ------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| A trucking app                 | B3Hub is a marketplace — carriers are one side of three                                                 |
| A SaaS startup                 | Product-market fit is physical + digital together, not pure software                                    |
| A sustainability platform      | ESG is a feature (CO₂ reporting, waste certificates) — not the lead                                     |
| A "marketplace for everything" | Narrow and deep in construction logistics, not broad                                                    |
| A Latvian government portal    | Must feel modern, fast, and private-sector sharp                                                        |
| Schüttflix clone               | Schüttflix is Germany-scale. B3 Laukumi is the physical network competitors can't copy. Lead with that. |
| "The B3 Group"                 | Don't add "Group" — it signals holding company complexity you don't have yet                            |
| Three unrelated companies      | B3Hub, B3 Laukumi, and B3 Būvniecība must visually read as family, not strangers                        |

## Brand Promise (Internal North Star)

> Every tonne that moves through B3 — whether ordered online, picked up at a B3 Laukumi, or moved by B3 Būvniecība — is tracked, documented, and settled automatically.
> That promise doesn't change, no matter what feature we build next.
